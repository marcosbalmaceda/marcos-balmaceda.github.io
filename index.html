Marcos Balmaceda

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Business Economics at the Department of Management, Strategy and Innovation (MSI) at KU Leuven. My advisors are Stijn Kelchtermans and Bart Leten. During 2026, I visited the Department of Economics & Decision Sciences at HEC Paris, hosted by Thomas Åstebro.

My research focuses on how technological change transforms business strategy and entrepreneurial opportunities. I am particularly interested in the governance of new technologies, the design of market mechanisms that enable innovation, and the distributional impacts of technological adoption across different contexts. Through empirical approaches combining econometrics and field experiments, I explore how institutional arrangements and strategic decisions shape market efficiency and access to opportunities.

I was born and grew up in Chile, where I gained extensive experience in policy and research roles across various institutions before transitioning to academia.

Research interests:

  • Primary: Strategy, Entrepreneurship, Technology & Innovation, Platform Markets, Applied Microeconomics.
  • Methodological: Applied Econometrics, Causal Inference, Field Experiments, Artificial Intelligence.
